Global Effects of Technology

This article has divided the discussion of the Global Effects of Technology into 4 categories: Conquering Language Barriers, Transforming Global Commerce, Methods of Conducting Business and Establishing Personal Connections.

In the first topic of Conquering Language Barriers discusses how with the computing power of smart phones and having technology more accessible to a large group of people, this in turn means that more and more people have the ability to communicate fluently with one another. With many services like Google Translate becoming more and more popular it allows people to translate what they want to say intomany different languages and also recieve a message in a different language with that of which you are familiar with and translate it into your native language.

Another area of our lives that technology is having a huge impact on is the way we buy and sell goods. Technology has allowed for e-commerce to boom in popularity. The important thing that we must know as consumers is to keep the competition fair between online purchases and local store purchases. It is our duty as consumers to ensure that technology doesn't entirely get rid of the way we buy and sell goods in person as well. Technology should help us as people not cause more problems. Technology is also having a huge impact on the way we conduct business with one another. With services like Skype and GoToMeeting, it is giving us opportunities to attend board meetings and conference calls while presenting our info to a large group of people that do not have to be in the same room as one another. This is a huge impact because it allows people to communicate easier with people they may not normally talk to due to constraints of distance.

Finally, the author discusses the problems that technology is creating on the establishment of personal connections. Technology is causing many humans to lose their social skills, there is a different set of skills for being able to talk to a person face to face versus being able to communicate through words over a text or email. If we continue to rely only on communication through technology then we will eventually lose our face to face communication skills, therefore hurting the human race and causing us to be entirely technology dependent.
